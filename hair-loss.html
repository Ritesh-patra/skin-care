Understanding Hair Loss & PRP Treatment

Hair loss affects millions of people worldwide and can significantly impact self-confidence and quality of life. Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a revolutionary treatment that uses your own blood's healing properties to stimulate hair follicle growth. PRP was first used in 1987 in open heart surgery and has since been recognized for its immense potential in treating hair loss with natural, lasting results.

What is PRP Therapy?

Platelet Rich Plasma is a concentrated form of your own platelets, obtained from your blood. Rich in growth factors, PRP stimulates hair follicle stem cells to divide and thicken miniature hairs. Combined with medical treatment and oral medications, PRP treatments boost re-growth and restore much more hair than was thought possible before. The treatment is safe, natural, and uses your body's own healing mechanisms.

How PRP Works for Hair Loss

  • Stimulates hair follicle stem cells to divide and multiply
  • Increases blood flow to hair follicles
  • Promotes thickening of miniature hairs
  • Extends the growth phase of hair cycle
  • Reduces hair shedding and breakage
  • Improves overall hair density and volume
  • Works best when combined with medical treatment

The PRP Treatment Process

During your PRP treatment session, a small amount of blood is drawn from your arm. This blood is then placed in a centrifuge machine that separates the platelets from other blood components, creating a concentrated plasma rich in growth factors. This PRP is then carefully injected into the scalp areas experiencing hair loss. The entire process takes about 30-45 minutes and is minimally invasive.

Preparation & Aftercare

Before Treatment:

  • Avoid blood thinners for 1 week prior
  • Stay hydrated and maintain good nutrition
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before treatment
  • Inform us about any medications you're taking

After Treatment:

  • Avoid washing hair for 24 hours
  • Don't use harsh hair products for 1 week
  • Avoid strenuous exercise for 48 hours
  • Take prescribed medications as recommended
  • Attend follow-up sessions as scheduled
