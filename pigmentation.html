Understanding Pigmentation & Tattoo Removal

Uneven skin tone is caused by over production of the skin pigment, melanin, commonly caused by over exposure to the Sun, hormonal changes, visible blood vessels (Rosacea), or inflammation from acne. Our new-age laser techniques are a 'class-apart' in erasing pigmentation with little risk of scarring. The laser light breaks pigment particles into tiny fragments which are then washed out through the body's natural cleansing mechanism.

Causes of Pigmentation Issues

  • Excessive sun exposure and UV radiation
  • Hormonal changes and imbalances
  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation from acne
  • Melasma (pregnancy mask) and chloasma
  • Freckles and age spots
  • Rosacea and visible blood vessels
  • Genetic predisposition to pigmentation

Advanced Laser Technology for Pigmentation

Our advanced laser techniques use targeted light energy to break down melanin and pigment particles. The laser light is absorbed by the pigment, causing it to fragment into tiny particles. These particles are then naturally eliminated through the body's lymphatic system. This process is highly effective for treating melasma, dark spots, freckles, and other pigmentation concerns.

Tattoo Removal Process

Our Q-switched laser technology is highly effective for tattoo removal. The laser breaks down tattoo ink particles into smaller fragments that the body can eliminate naturally. Multiple sessions are typically required depending on tattoo size, color, and depth. Black and dark-colored tattoos respond best to laser treatment.

Preparation & Aftercare

Before Treatment:

  • Avoid sun exposure for 2 weeks prior
  • Stop using retinoids 1 week before
  • Avoid waxing and chemical treatments
  • Inform us about any medications

After Treatment:

  • Apply sunscreen (SPF 50+) daily for 4 weeks
  • Avoid direct sun exposure and tanning beds
  • Use gentle, fragrance-free moisturizers
  • Avoid makeup for 48 hours after treatment
  • Don't pick or scratch treated areas
